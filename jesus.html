Who Is Jesus?

1- He was a Jew. John 4 verse 9, Then saith the woman of Samaria unto him, How is it that thou, being a Jew, askest drink of me, which am a woman of Samaria? For the Jews have no dealings with the Samaritans.

2- He was born in Judea. Mathew 2 verse 1, ¶ Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judaea in the days of Herod the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to Jerusalem,

3- He was from Judah’s tribe. Mathew 1 verse 1, The book of the generation of Jesus [the] Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham. Abraham begat Isaac; and Isaac begat Jacob; and Jacob begat Judas (Judah) and his brethren;

4- He was the Jewish Messiah. Mark 8 verse 29, And he saith unto them, But whom say ye that I am? And Peter answereth and saith unto him, Thou art the Christ. Mathew 16 verse 20, Then charged he his disciples that they should tell no man that he was Jesus the Christ.

5- He was born during Tabernacles. John 1 verse 14, And the Word became flesh, and tabernacled among us (and we beheld his glory, glory as of the only begotten from the Father), full of grace and truth. • There is much evidence that Messiah was born at Tabernacles (Boothes/ Sukkot) on September 11th in 3 B.C. The key to calculating the date of the birth of Messiah is Luke 1 verse 5 where we learn that Zechariah the father of Yochanan was a priest of the course of Abijah. • Jesus was born in a “stable”. The Hebrew word for "stable" is "sukkah" (as in Gen. 33 verse 17) • If Jesus was born on the first day of Tabernacles then he would have been circumcised on the "eigth great day" the festival following Tabernacles. • This would mean that Jesus, the light of the world was conceived by the Spirit during the festival of lights, AKA Hanukkah. Genesis 1 verse 3, 1 verse 14, • This means also that John, His Jewish cousin was born 6 months earlier at Passover which is when Jesus died as our Passover Lamb of God (Mathew 3 verse 1,In those days came John the Baptist, preaching in the wilderness of Judaea, Mathew 26 verse 2 Ye know that after two days is the feast of the Passover, and the Son of man is betrayed to be crucified.)

6- His name is Yeshua (ישוע). Mathew 1 verse 21, And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name JESUS verse for he shall save his people from their sins. Mathew 1 verse 25, And knew her not till she had brought forth her firstborn son verse and he called his name JESUS. Other little known facts for consideration 1. Yeshua means “salvation” in Hebrew. In Mathew 1 you see it capitalized in the KJV for a similar reason as YHVH is all caps as LORD when translated. Mathew 1 verse 21 in English makes zero sense when you use the name “Jesus”. 2. Jesus’ ministry according to the Bible was 70 weeks, not 3 ½ years. Many scholars changed their belief to 2 years based on Biblical evidence. But even 2 years is too long when you line up the chronological gospels, from Tishri One in A.D.27 to Tishri One in A.D.28 Sabbatical year. Jesus was 30 years old (30 years after September 11th in 3 B.C) when He started His 70-week ministry.

7- His brother was Jacob. James 1 verse 1, James [Iakobos], a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus [the] Christ, to the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad, greeting. Jesus actually had several siblings recorded in the Bible. “Isn’t this the carpenter’s son? Isn’t his mother’s name Mary, and aren’t his brothers James, Joseph, Simon and Judas? Aren’t all his sisters with us? Where then did this man get all these things?” (Matthew 13 verse 55–56)
