Calendar Dates

🕊️ Fulfillment

  • Passover: Jesus' death on the cross is seen as the fulfillment (happened on the same-day, anniversary) of the Passover lamb, where his blood provides redemption.

  • Feast of Unleavened Bread: Jesus' burial is fulfilled (happened on the same-day, anniversary) during this feast, which symbolizes his sinless life being buried with him as the "bread of life".
  • Feast of First Fruits: His resurrection on this day marked him (happened on the same-day, anniversary) as the firstfruits of those who will be resurrected.
  • Pentecost: The Holy Spirit was given to believers 50 days after the resurrection, fulfilling (happened on the same-day, anniversary) the promise of the new covenant sealed in Jesus' blood. This was/ is also the anniversary of the first Covenant at Mt. Sinai.
  • Feasts fulfilled by Jesus' second coming:
  • Feast of Trumpets: This is seen as a signal for his return, potentially marking the rapture of believers.
  • Day of Atonement: This feast is associated with the final judgment and his second coming as king.
  • Feast of Tabernacles: This represents the 1,000-year Messianic kingdom on earth, which will be established when he returns as king

Holy Days / Feasts Explained

  • Evening of Wed, Oct 1, 2025 – Thu, Oct 2, 2025
  • Sukkot (Feast of Tabernacles): Starts the evening of Monday, October 6, 2025, and concludes at nightfall on Monday, October 13, 2025
  • Shemini Atzeret: Follows Sukkot, beginning the evening of Monday, October 13, 2025, and ending at nightfall on Tuesday, October 14, 2025

    🕊️ Spring Feasts

  • Pesach (Passover): The first spring feast, commemorating the liberation of the Israelites from Egyptian slavery.:
  • Matzot (Unleavened Bread): Follows Passover and celebrates the hasty departure from Egypt, with no time for bread to rise.
  • Bikkurim (First Fruits): Celebrates the first harvest and the resurrection of the Messiah as the first fruits from the dead.

    • 🕊️ Summer Feast

  • Pentecost: Shavuot (Pentecost): Also known as the Feast of Weeks, it celebrates the second grain harvest and commemorates the giving of the Torah to Moses.

    • 🕊️ Fall Feasts

  • Yom Teruah (Trumpets): Also called Rosh Hashanah, it marks the Jewish New Year and is a time of sounding trumpets.
  • Yom Kippur (Day of Atonement): A day of fasting, repentance, and atonement for sins.
  • Sukkot (Feast of Tabernacles/Booths): A week-long festival commemorating the Israelites' 40 years of wandering in the desert, living in temporary shelters.

Witness of the People of God

Scripture has been recognized, confessed, prayed, translated, and preserved within the life of God’s people across generations. This witness reflects lived faithfulness rather than scholarly analysis.

  • Canon — These writings were received as Scripture through use and trust, not imposed by force.
  • Creeds — Core truths were confessed publicly, often under persecution, as a summary of shared faith.
  • Prayer & Liturgy — Scripture shaped daily prayer, worship, and communal life.
  • Translation — God’s Word crossed languages so that people might hear and understand.
  • Preservation — The text endured because it mattered enough to be copied, protected, and suffered for.

This witness reflects how Scripture was lived before it was analyzed.
